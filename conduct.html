Code of Conduct

Last updated: August 2025

This Code of Conduct outlines our community standards and expectations for everyone who participates in the Open Flame Project and Burner Profile system.

Our Community Values

What We Stand For

The Open Flame Project is built on the foundation of Burning Man's 10 Principles, adapted for our digital community space:

  • Radical Inclusion: Everyone is welcome
  • Gifting: Sharing skills and knowledge freely
  • Decommodification: Community over commerce
  • Radical Self-reliance: Taking responsibility for yourself
  • Radical Self-expression: Authentic participation
  • Communal Effort: Working together for shared goals
  • Civic Responsibility: Caring for our community
  • Leaving No Trace: Positive impact only
  • Participation: Active engagement over passive consumption
  • Immediacy: Present, authentic connections

For Volunteers

When Participating in Events

  • Be reliable: Follow through on commitments you make
  • Communicate clearly: Let organisers know if plans change
  • Be prepared: Come ready to contribute your best effort
  • Stay flexible: Adapt to changing needs and circumstances
  • Support your team: Help create a positive working environment
  • Learn and grow: Use opportunities to develop new skills

For Event Organisers

When Using the Burner Profile System

  • Respect privacy: Only access profiles for legitimate volunteer coordination
  • Communicate clearly: Be specific about roles, expectations, and time commitments
  • Be inclusive: Consider volunteers from diverse backgrounds and experience levels
  • Provide support: Offer training, mentorship, and resources
  • Give recognition: Acknowledge contributions and celebrate achievements
  • Foster growth: Help volunteers develop skills and advance their involvement

Unacceptable Behaviour

We Do Not Tolerate

Harassment and Discrimination:

  • Harassment based on identity, background, or characteristics
  • Discriminatory language or behaviour
  • Unwelcome sexual attention or advances
  • Bullying, intimidation, or threats
  • Stalking or persistent unwanted contact

Misuse of the Platform:

  • Using volunteer information for commercial purposes
  • Sharing personal data without consent
  • Creating fake or misleading profiles
  • Spamming or excessive promotional content
  • Attempting to hack or compromise the system

Specific Examples

Unacceptable behaviour includes, but is not limited to:

  • Personal attacks: Insulting, demeaning, or attacking individuals
  • Hate speech: Language that promotes hatred or violence against groups
  • Doxxing: Sharing someone's private information without consent
  • Impersonation: Pretending to be someone else or misrepresenting yourself
  • Exploitation: Taking advantage of volunteers or their labour
  • Retaliation: Punishing someone for reporting misconduct

Accessibility and Inclusion

Creating an Accessible Community

With 58% of our community identifying as neurodivergent, accessibility is central to our values:

Communication Accessibility:

  • Use clear, direct language
  • Provide information in multiple formats
  • Allow extra time for responses
  • Respect different communication styles
  • Offer alternative ways to participate

Physical and Sensory Accessibility:

  • Consider mobility and physical access needs
  • Provide sensory-friendly options
  • Respect energy and capacity limitations
  • Offer flexible participation options
  • Create quiet spaces and breaks

Supporting Neurodivergent Community Members

  • Respect different processing styles: Some people need more time to think or respond
  • Be explicit about expectations: Clear instructions and timelines help everyone
  • Offer multiple communication channels: Text, voice, video, in-person options
  • Provide structure and predictability: Clear agendas, consistent processes
  • Celebrate diverse strengths: Recognise different ways of contributing

Reporting and Response

How to Report Issues

Reporting Channels

  • Email: open.flame.project.nsw@gmail.com
  • Subject line: "Code of Conduct Report"
  • Slack: Direct message to Community Data Stewards
  • Anonymous: Use a temporary email or ask someone to report for you

What to Include

  • Description of what happened
  • When and where it occurred
  • Who was involved (if comfortable sharing)
  • Any evidence (screenshots, messages, etc.)
  • How you'd like us to respond

Our Response Process

1. Acknowledgment (24 hours)

We'll confirm we received your report and provide a timeline for our response.

2. Investigation (3-7 days)

We'll gather information, speak with relevant parties, and assess the situation.

3. Decision and Action

We'll determine appropriate consequences and implement them fairly and consistently.

4. Follow-up

We'll check in with affected parties and monitor the situation to prevent recurrence.

Consequences

Progressive Response

We believe in education and growth, but also in protecting our community. Consequences may include:

Minor Violations

  • Education: Discussion about community standards
  • Guidance: Help understanding appropriate behaviour
  • Mediation: Facilitated conversation between parties
  • Apology: Acknowledgment of harm and commitment to change

Serious Violations

  • Temporary suspension: Time away from the platform
  • Restricted access: Limited participation privileges
  • Permanent ban: Removal from the community
  • Event notification: Informing relevant event organisers

Factors We Consider

  • Severity: How much harm was caused
  • Intent: Whether the behaviour was deliberate
  • Pattern: If this is part of ongoing problematic behaviour
  • Impact: How it affected individuals and the community
  • Response: How the person responds when confronted
  • Community safety: What's needed to protect others

Appeals Process

If You Disagree with a Decision

You have the right to appeal any consequences imposed:

  • Submit an appeal: Email open.flame.project.nsw@gmail.com with "Appeal" in the subject line
  • Provide new information: Share any relevant details we may not have considered
  • Independent review: A different Community Data Steward will review your case
  • Timeline: Appeals are reviewed within 14 days
  • Final decision: The appeal decision is final
