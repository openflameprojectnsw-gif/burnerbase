Code of Conduct

Our commitment

The Open Flame Project creates a safe, inclusive space for all NSW Burns community members. Everyone deserves to participate without harassment, discrimination, or harm.

Our values

Respect

Treat everyone with dignity, regardless of their background, identity, or experience level.

Inclusion

Welcome people of all abilities, identities, and circumstances.

Consent

Always get clear permission before sharing someone's information or contacting them.

Accountability

Take responsibility for your actions and their impact on others.

Community first

Put the wellbeing of the community above personal interests.

Expected behaviour

When using the Burnerbase:

  • Provide accurate information about your skills and experience
  • Respect other people's privacy settings and boundaries
  • Use appropriate language in all communications
  • Report problems or concerns promptly

When contacting volunteers:

  • Only contact people who have opted in to hear from your event
  • Be clear about what you're asking and what's involved
  • Respect "no" as a complete answer
  • Follow up professionally and promptly

When volunteering:

  • Show up when you commit to something
  • Communicate clearly if your availability changes
  • Follow the specific event's safety and conduct policies
  • Support other volunteers and create an inclusive environment

Unacceptable behaviour

We do not tolerate:

  • Harassment, bullying, or intimidation
  • Discrimination based on identity, ability, or background
  • Sharing personal information without consent
  • Misrepresenting skills, experience, or availability
  • Using the platform for commercial purposes or spam
  • Behaviour that makes others feel unsafe or unwelcome

Specific examples:

  • Contacting volunteers who haven't opted in to your event
  • Pressuring people to volunteer when they've said no
  • Making assumptions about someone's abilities based on their identity
  • Sharing volunteer contact details with third parties
  • Creating fake profiles or misrepresenting yourself

Accessibility and inclusion

We actively support:

  • Neurodivergent community members
  • People with disabilities
  • LGBTIQA+ community members
  • People from diverse cultural backgrounds
  • People with different economic circumstances
  • People new to the Burns community

This means:

  • Using clear, simple language in all communications
  • Providing information in multiple formats when possible
  • Respecting different communication styles and needs
  • Creating opportunities for people with varying availability
  • Not making assumptions about what people can or want to do

Reporting concerns

If someone violates this Code of Conduct:

  1. Direct approach: If you feel safe, tell the person their behaviour is not okay
  2. Report to us: Email conduct@openflameproject.org with details
  3. Report to event organisers: If it happens during an event, use their reporting process
  4. Emergency: If someone is in immediate danger, call 000

When reporting, include:

  • What happened (be as specific as possible)
  • When and where it happened
  • Who was involved
  • Any evidence (screenshots, messages, etc.)
  • How you'd like us to respond

Our response process

We will:

  • Acknowledge your report within 48 hours
  • Investigate thoroughly and fairly
  • Keep your information confidential
  • Take appropriate action based on our findings
  • Follow up with you about the outcome

Possible actions include:

  • Education and guidance for minor issues
  • Formal warnings for serious violations
  • Temporary suspension from the platform
  • Permanent removal for severe or repeated violations
  • Reporting to relevant authorities if required by law

Appeals

If you disagree with our decision:

  • Email appeals@openflameproject.org within 14 days
  • Provide new information or explain why you think the decision was wrong
  • A different Community Data Steward will review your case
  • We'll respond within 7 days with our final decision

This Code applies to all interactions related to the Open Flame Project, including the Burnerbase platform, community channels, and events where our volunteers participate.

We're committed to creating a space where everyone can contribute safely and meaningfully to our community.
