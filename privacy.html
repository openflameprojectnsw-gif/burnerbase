Privacy Policy

Last updated: August 2025

This Privacy Policy explains how the Open Flame Project collects, uses, and protects your personal information when you create a Burner profile.

Simple Version

Your Privacy in Plain English

  • Your data stays in the community - we never sell or commercialise your information
  • You control who sees your profile - only approved event organisers you choose
  • You can update or delete anytime - your profile, your choice
  • We follow Australian privacy laws - including the Privacy Act 1988
  • Community governance - decisions made by Community Data Stewards, not commercial interests
  • Transparent operations - we publish regular reports on how data is used

What Information We Collect

Profile Information

When you create your Burner profile, we collect:

  • Basic details: Name, email, location, contact preferences
  • Burns experience: Events attended, roles held, experience level
  • Skills and talents: What you can contribute to events
  • Career goals: How you want to grow in the community
  • Availability: Time commitment and scheduling preferences
  • Accessibility needs: Support requirements for inclusive participation

Technical Information

We automatically collect some technical information to improve the service:

  • Website usage: Pages visited, time spent, browser type
  • Device information: Operating system, screen size (for accessibility)
  • IP address: For security and spam prevention

Communication Records

We keep records of:

  • Support emails: When you contact us for help
  • Profile updates: Changes you make to your information
  • Privacy settings: Your choices about who can contact you

How We Use Your Information

Primary Uses

  • Volunteer matching: Connect you with relevant opportunities
  • Event coordination: Help organisers find skilled volunteers
  • Career development: Support your growth in the community
  • Community building: Foster connections between members

Administrative Uses

  • Platform improvement: Enhance user experience and accessibility
  • Security: Prevent spam and protect against misuse
  • Support: Respond to your questions and requests
  • Compliance: Meet legal and community governance requirements

Who Can See Your Information

You Control Access

Your Burner profile is only visible to:

Approved Event Organisers

Only organisers of events you've specifically approved can search and view your profile. You choose which events can contact you.

Community Data Stewards

Appointed community members who manage the platform and ensure it serves community interests. They're bound by strict confidentiality agreements.

Technical Support

Ryan (T-Dog) from Undiscovered Events and authorised technical staff, only for platform maintenance and support purposes.

What We Never Do

  • Never sell your data to commercial organisations
  • Never share with marketers or advertising companies
  • Never make profiles public without your explicit consent
  • Never use for profit - this is a community service
  • Never share with government unless legally required

Data Security

How We Protect Your Information

Technical Security:

  • Encrypted data transmission (HTTPS)
  • Secure database storage
  • Regular security updates and monitoring
  • Access controls and authentication
  • Regular backups and disaster recovery

Operational Security:

  • Limited access to authorised personnel only
  • Confidentiality agreements for all staff
  • Regular security training and awareness
  • Incident response procedures
  • Community oversight and governance

Data Breach Response

If a security incident occurs, we will:

  • Immediate response: Secure the system and assess the impact
  • Notify affected users: Within 72 hours via email
  • Report to authorities: As required by Australian privacy laws
  • Community transparency: Publish a public report on what happened
  • Prevent recurrence: Implement additional security measures

Community Governance

Community Data Stewards

The Burner Profile system is governed by appointed Community Data Stewards who:

Their Responsibilities:

  • Protect community privacy and data rights
  • Make decisions about platform access and usage
  • Ensure community benefit over commercial interests
  • Oversee compliance with privacy policies
  • Handle disputes and community concerns

Accountability Measures:

  • Regular community reports on data usage
  • Transparent decision-making processes
  • Community input on major policy changes
  • Annual review of governance practices
  • Open communication channels for feedback

Transparency Reports

We publish quarterly reports including:

  • Number of profiles created and active
  • Which events have access and how they use it
  • Privacy requests received and how they were handled
  • Security incidents and responses
  • Platform improvements and community feedback

Integration with Undiscovered Events

Technical Partnership

Your Burner profile will be integrated with the Undiscovered Events platform. This means:

Data Sharing:

  • Your profile data is stored on Undiscovered Events servers
  • Same privacy protections apply to all data
  • Community governance extends to all data usage
  • No commercial use of volunteer information

Your Controls:

  • Same privacy settings and access controls
  • Ability to export or delete your data
  • Separate from any commercial ticketing data
  • Community oversight of all integrations

Legal Compliance

Australian Privacy Act 1988

We comply with the Australian Privacy Act 1988 and the Australian Privacy Principles (APPs), including:

  • APP 1: Open and transparent management of personal information
  • APP 3: Collection of solicited personal information
  • APP 5: Notification of the collection of personal information
  • APP 6: Use or disclosure of personal information
  • APP 11: Security of personal information
  • APP 12: Access to personal information
  • APP 13: Correction of personal information

Data Retention

We keep your information:

  • Active profiles: As long as you want to participate
  • Inactive profiles: 2 years after last activity, then archived
  • Deleted profiles: Permanently removed within 30 days
  • Support records: 3 years for legal compliance
  • Security logs: 1 year for incident investigation

Changes to This Policy

How We Handle Updates

If we need to change this privacy policy:

  • Community consultation: Major changes discussed with Community Data Stewards
  • Advance notice: 30 days notice via email for significant changes
  • Clear explanation: What's changing and why
  • Your choice: Option to delete your profile if you disagree
  • Version history: Previous versions available for reference

Contact Us About Privacy

Privacy Questions

For any privacy-related questions or concerns:

  • Email: open.flame.project.nsw@gmail.com
  • Subject line: "Privacy Query" for faster response
  • Response time: Within 48 hours
  • Escalation: Community Data Stewards if needed

External Complaints

If you're not satisfied with our response, you can contact:

  • Office of the Australian Information Commissioner (OAIC)
  • Website: www.oaic.gov.au
  • Phone: 1300 363 992
  • Email: enquiries@oaic.gov.au

Summary

Our Privacy Commitment

The Open Flame Project exists to serve the NSW Burns community. Your privacy and data protection are fundamental to this mission. We're committed to:

  • Community ownership of data and decision-making
  • Transparency in all our operations
  • Your control over your personal information
  • Security that protects your data
  • Accountability to the community we serve

Questions? Contact us at open.flame.project.nsw@gmail.com
