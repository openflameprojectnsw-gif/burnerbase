About the Open Flame Project

Community-owned technology to mobilise our collective potential

üî• Our Mission

To address the challenges facing the NSW Burns ecosystem and wider Australian Burner community through technology and community coordination.

The 2025 NSW Burns Community Survey revealed significant challenges: fragmented volunteer coordination, communication barriers, and untapped community potential. The Open Flame Project uses proven technology solutions to tackle these specific issues.

üõ°Ô∏è Privacy & Trust

We understand that sharing your information requires trust. Here's why the Open Flame Project is trustworthy and how we protect your privacy:

‚úÖ What We Do

  • Community ownership: Governed by Community Data Stewards, not commercial interests
  • Transparent operations: Regular public reports on data use and platform governance
  • Privacy controls: You choose which events can see your profile
  • Data portability: Export your data anytime in standard formats
  • Australian compliance: Full adherence to Privacy Act 1988
  • Community accountability: Direct access to leadership and open feedback channels

‚ùå What We Don't Do

  • Sell your data: Never commercialised or sold to third parties
  • Track across platforms: No social media integration or cross-platform tracking
  • Share without permission: Only approved event organisers you choose can see your profile
  • Keep data forever: Delete your profile anytime and it's gone permanently
  • Make decisions in secret: All major decisions discussed openly with community
  • Lock you in: Easy to update, pause, or delete your involvement anytime

ü§ù Partnership with Undiscovered Events

Why partner with a commercial platform? This partnership actually strengthens your privacy protection:

  • Proven infrastructure: Professional hosting and security already trusted by NSW Burners
  • Local expertise: Ryan (T-Dog) is a respected community member, not a distant corporation
  • Community Data Stewards: Independent oversight of all data practices
  • Strict agreements: Legal contracts protecting community data rights
  • Exit strategy: Clear plan for data portability if partnership ever ends

Think of it like renting a secure building (Undiscovered Events) but having your own locks, security guards, and rules about who can enter (Community Data Stewards).

üöÄ Start Small, Build Trust

You don't have to share everything at once. Here's how to get started safely:

  1. Start with basic info: Share just your name, general location, and a few key skills
  2. Choose one event: Opt into just one event you trust to start
  3. Build gradually: Add more information as you become comfortable
  4. Stay in control: Regularly review your privacy settings
